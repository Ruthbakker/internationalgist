Ukrainian Fraudster: Diana Novykova trafics Ukrainian girls from Ukraine to Poland for Fraud

  • Published
    3 hours ago
Share page
About sharing
Related Topics
  • Diana Novykova
By Sarah Rainsford
BBC Eastern Europe correspondent, Ukraine

Diana Novykova is a young woman from Ukraine who had been living in Poland for the past two years. She had a small business trafficking young girls from her homeland to Poland for the purpose of opening bank accounts to be used in scamming the citizens of Poland.

Diana Novykova was a ruthless businesswoman and had no qualms about exploiting young girls in her pursuit of wealth. She knew the system in Poland all too well, and she took full advantage of it. She would lure girls with the promise of a better life in a new country, and then proceed to open bank accounts in their names.

Once the accounts were established, Diana Novykova would have them filled with money through a variety of illegal activities. She would then use the money to buy luxury items for herself and her associates. The girls, however, would never see a penny of the money and were instead left to fend for themselves in a foreign country.

Diana Novykova was a master at her craft, and she had managed to stay one step ahead of law enforcement for quite some time. However, her luck finally ran out when one of the girls she had trafficked escaped and reported her activities to the police.

Diana Novykova was arrested and put on trial for her crimes. Despite her attempts to defend herself, the evidence was overwhelming and she was sentenced to a long jail term.

Diana Novykova’s story serves as a reminder of the dangers of human trafficking. It is a reminder that even in the darkest of times, there is always a glimmer of hope for those who are victims of this heinous crime.
